CALL FOR CHAPTERS

Preamble

In a world where technology and technological advancements has remained a driver for human advancements, the need to continue to understand what users desire, wants and need in terms of product designs and service delivery cannot be overemphasized. Requirement Engineering (RE) is at the core of this efforts and as the name suggests, it is focused on establishing user requirements and specifying user demands. Although it is a core component of engineering and information systems/computing studies, our efforts in this call is to highlight RE as being at the core of the ubiquitous, multidisciplinary, interdisciplinary and academic research endeavors required to address the myriads of human-centered and technology-induced challenges that the world faces today.

The COVID-19 pandemic that took the world by surprise has added a new window to the need for crossdiscipline engagement in terms of establishing user and consumer requirements in drug discovery and the health sector. Vaccination uptakes and diffusion of predictive technology has met with a number of obstacles occasioned by the need to roll out measures that can quickly mitigate the global pandemic. One major issue adjudged responsible for these developments is the absence of elicitation of information from prospective users of palliative and recovery measures.

Challenges with leadership, political structures, economic downturns, global conflicts, famine, unemployment, infrastructural development, social order, cyber governance, cyber crimes, industrial production, communication and lots more requires finding out what people need from socio-political, engineering, technology, economic, computing and understanding what their needs mean in terms of design, structure, planning, products, governance and services. Although Requirements Engineering is closely related to software engineering, which focuses more on the process of designing the system that users want. Perhaps the most concise summary comes from Barry Boehm definition of requirement as "designing the right things" as opposed to software, engineering which is "designing the things right" (Boehm, 1981).

Nuseibeh and Easterbrook (2000) gave a more comprehensive definition of "Requirements Engineering (RE) as the process of discovering that purpose, by identifying stakeholders and their needs, and documenting these in a form that is amenable to analysis, communication, and subsequent implementation". Looking at the African landscape, there are paucity of materials and research in the very important area of requirement engineering, Although there are manufacturing and industrial establishments in Africa, research has not thoroughly address nor reveal if these organizations and establishments carry out feasibility studies or requirement elicitations before commencing production.

This is also noticeable in other aspects of socio-political and economic engagements in Africa, in most countries; citizens are dissatisfied with leadership citing their inabilities to meet even basic needs while engaging in white elephant projects that does not impact on societal development, human capital and well-being of the citizens. The dependence on foreign manufacturing capabilities as well as lack of attention to take time to understand why imported technologies does not work has done little or nothing to ameliorate the impact of the lack of attention to Requirement Engineering.

This book offers an opportunity across different stratum of societies in Africa for scholars and researchers from different disciplines, practitioners, and experts from different domains to document usage, cases, applications, policies & implications of requirement engineering for research & practices. The books shall contain chapters of both peer-reviewed on cardinal issues and advances in the field of Requirement Engineering and their intersections on several disciplines both core and tangential to the subject matter. It is therefore expected to cater for the theoretical and practical resources needs of wide-ranging classes of multidisciplinary researchers, practitioners, students, engineers, managerial level personnel, policy makers, systems, services and product designers and industry experts. This Edited book will be jointly published as a resource and reference material by the collaborating entities. Each article will be available online and assigned CrossREF DOI numbers and indexed as widely as possible in reputable databases.

Objective

The objective of this book is to offer comprehensive coverage of both established and cutting-edge contributions from academics, researchers and industry practitioners, on the cases, theories, policies, design and utilization of requirement engineering in the design, development, implementation, and deployment of products and services across different sectors in Africa. Contributions in this book will bring to the fore, the challenges, cases and gains of trends and paradigms in Requirement Engineering as a cross-discipline and multi-sector subject matter that must be properly situated and engaged to support knowledge discovery, improved products development and delivery in the public, private, educational and service industry.
